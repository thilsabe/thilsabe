## About Me

I'm a computational biologist with 10+ years of experience designing and deploying 
scalable genomics and multi-omics pipelines in translational research environments. 
My work sits at the intersection of statistical modeling, machine learning, and 
biological interpretation — turning high-dimensional molecular data into clear, 
testable hypotheses about aging and disease.

Currently a **Postdoctoral Fellow** at the **Salk Institute for Biological Studies**, 
where I lead integrative analyses across transcriptomic, proteomic, and genomic 
datasets and develop reproducible end-to-end workflows on Linux/HPC systems.

Previously at the **Buck Institute for Research on Aging** (Campisi Lab, Kapahi & 
Brem Labs), where I completed my Ph.D. in Biology of Aging and conducted postdoctoral 
research on biomarker discovery, multi-omics integration, and the genetics of 
diet-dependent lifespan and healthspan.

## What I Work On

- **Single-cell & bulk transcriptomics** — scRNA-seq (Seurat), differential expression, 
  WGCNA/hdWGCNA co-expression network analysis, trajectory analysis
- **RNA-seq preprocessing** — STAR, HISAT2, GSNAP, FASTQ QC (Trim Galore, Trimmomatic, 
  fastp), samtools, rMATS splicing analysis
- **Variant analysis** — SNV/CNV/SV calling, WES/WGS/array association analysis, QC pipelines
- **Statistical & causal modeling** — survival analysis, random forest modeling, mixed models
- **Multi-omics integration** — connecting metabolomic, transcriptomic, proteomic, 
  and genomic layers to biological conclusions
- **Pipeline development** — reproducible, modular workflows in Python, R, and shell 
  on HPC/cloud environments, Git version control
